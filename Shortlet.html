Overview

A design-thinking approach to a new kind of investing.

Real estate is one of the safest ways to build wealth, and one of the hardest to access. Shortlet Lagos set out to lower that barrier: fractional, tokenized ownership of premium shortlet apartments, starting at $50, with property management handled end to end.

Because the product asks people to trust a new model (Web3 + real estate), I structured the work around the five stages of design thinking, keeping real user needs at the centre of every decision. [Replace with your own overview.]

02Define

The real problem wasn't access. It was trust.

Synthesizing the research, I reframed the challenge from "how do we let people invest" to "how do we make a brand-new, blockchain-based model feel safe, simple, and worth acting on."

Problem statement

First-time investors want the wealth-building power of Lagos real estate, but the cost, complexity, and unfamiliarity of tokenized ownership stop them from starting. [Refine to your exact statement.]

Point of view

  • A cautious first-time investor needs to feel confident and in control because money and unfamiliar tech together feel risky.
  • A property owner needs transparency and fair terms because they won't tokenize an asset they can't trust.

How might we

  • Make returns, risk, and liquidity understandable at a glance?
  • Turn "Web3" from a barrier into a mark of security?
  • Give owners visible control over their tokenized property?
