Zenduit(Gofleet)  |  Fleet Management System

The admin layer that makes a telematics platform usable.

An end-to-end redesign of ZenduONE's admin portal, 16 scattered configuration areas reshaped into one guided, self-serve console for fleet, video, safety and dispatch.

My role

Lead Product Designer → research → IA → UI → prototype → handoff

Scope

3 roles

Admin · dispatcher · viewer, one IA

01The problem

Best-in-class telematics. Brutal to set up.

The data engine was world-class. But everything an admin had to configure before getting value lived in disconnected screens, no order, no guidance, no sense of what was done.

“I can track a truck across the country, but I can't onboard one without calling support.”

— Synthesized from 12 admin interviews

1

No map of "what do I configure?"

16 settings areas with no hierarchy. New admins couldn't tell foundations from optional modules, or what blocked go-live.

2

Zero sense of progress

Nothing showed how far through setup an admin was, what needed attention, or what was safe to skip.

3

Every module looked different

Feature-by-feature growth left inconsistent tables, forms and toggles, relearning the UI on every screen.

4

Telematics jargon everywhere

Driver-flow, coaching and device policies hid behind language only the implementation team understood.

02The core idea

One hub, organized around the admin's mental model, with progress built in.

I reframed 16 settings into three plain-language groups that map to how a fleet comes online: lay your foundations, turn on the modules you bought, then automate your process. A setup tracker makes "am I done?" answerable at a glance.

03The solution, in detail

01

Setup Your Fleet, the foundations

People, places and structure. These records are referenced everywhere downstream, so they had to be fast to manage in bulk and consistent to scan.

User Management

One source of truth for everyone in the fleet

Drivers, dispatchers, technicians and admins all lived in separate places before. I unified them into a single table where you can see role, group membership, status and last login at a glance, and act on people in bulk.

Access is governed by role-based Clearances, so an admin grants exactly the permissions a role needs without touching code.

Role-based clearancesBulk actionsGroup assignmentStatus & last login
Groups

Structure that mirrors how the business is run

Groups let a fleet model its own org, by region, division or contract, as a hierarchy. Every group surfaces its user, asset and rule counts, so an admin can see the shape and weight of the organization without drilling in.

Because everything downstream references groups, getting this layer right makes permissions, rules and reporting fall into place.

Nested hierarchyUser / asset / rule countsDrives permissions & rules
Locations

Every depot, yard and site in one registry

Physical places power geofencing, dispatch and reporting. The locations registry keeps each site's tags, contacts and on-site inventory together, so a place is defined once and reused everywhere it's needed.

Geofence-readyTags & contactsOn-site inventory
Tags

One labelling language across the platform

Rather than a separate category system per module, I designed a single shared tagging layer that applies to assets, drivers, locations and orders alike. Define a tag once and it becomes a filter, a grouping and a report dimension everywhere.

Cross-entityPowers filteringReusable everywhere
Custom Fields

Let each customer model their own data

No two fleets track the same things. Custom Fields let admins attach their own data to any entity and organize it into reusable sets, extending the platform to their workflow without engineering involvement.

Any entityGrouped into setsNo-code extensibility
Organization Details

Company-wide defaults, set once

This is where regional formats, system identifiers and platform-wide defaults are configured, the settings everything else inherits. An Incomplete badge here feeds straight into the setup tracker, so an unfinished profile is impossible to forget.

Regional defaultsSystem identifiersFeeds setup tracker
02

Configure Modules, per-product setup

Dense, settings-heavy surfaces made approachable: grouped sections, smart defaults, inline diagnostics, and a live mobile preview so admins see exactly what drivers will.

Driver Flow

Design the driver's day, and see it instantly

Admins configure the driver's start, and end-of-day flow, inspections, prompts, required fields on the left. The signature move: a live mobile preview on the right renders the exact app experience the driver will get, updating as each toggle changes.

It turned a blind, error-prone configuration into something an admin could confidently get right the first time.

Live mobile previewStep builderRequired-field control
Video & Safety

Camera policy without the complexity

One of the densest surfaces in the product. I grouped upload policy, live-stream defaults, retention rules and usage analytics into clear sections, and surfaced live device diagnostics so admins can confirm cameras are healthy before relying on them.

Upload & retention policyLive-stream defaultsDevice diagnostics
Work Settings

The operational identity of the business

Branding, operating hours, customer communications and marketplace integrations all sit together here, the settings that make the platform feel like an extension of the customer's own business rather than generic software.

BrandingOperating hoursCustomer comms
Indoor Tracking & Maps

Indoor and outdoor, one mental model

Floor plans, beacons and gateways are managed alongside GIS/outdoor maps in a single filterable list, so an admin configuring a warehouse and a yard uses the same patterns instead of learning two separate tools.

Floor plans & beaconsIndoor + outdoor typesUnified, filterable
03

Automation & Manage Process

The rules engine that runs the fleet once it's live, readable at a glance, with severity, trigger frequency and routing surfaced on each rule.

Rules

An automation engine you can actually read

A fleet may run 39+ rules across General, Safety, Dispatch, Maintenance and Bus. Instead of an opaque list, each rule wears its severity, queue routing and live trigger frequency on its face, so an admin can spot a noisy or misrouted rule instantly and filter by status or severity to tune it.

Severity at a glanceTrigger frequencyQueue routingStatus / severity filters
Coaching Management

Turn safety events into driver improvement

Coaching closes the loop on safety. Events route into a review queue with managed video review, and coaches are assigned to drivers automatically, turning raw alerts into a repeatable workflow that actually changes behaviour on the road.

Review queueManaged video reviewAuto coach assignment

04The system underneath

Consistency was the feature.

The reason 16 modules feel like one product: a token-based component library built alongside the screens. One persistent shell, dark left rail, breadcrumb, settings header, wraps every page.

Shared shell & nav

Left rail, breadcrumb and a save/cancel header that behave identically everywhere.

One data table

Search, filter, tag chips, status and pagination reused across 8+ list views.

Settings primitives

Section cards, labelled toggles, steppers and icon chips give every page the same rhythm.

Status language

Completed / Incomplete / Not Set Up and severity read the same in every context.

05Process & decisions

How I worked the problem.

01 · Discover

Interviews & ticket audit

Shadowed admins as they onboarded fleets, reviewed all 16 settings sections, and analyzed support tickets to identify setup issues.

02 · Define

IA & card sort

Organized 16 modules into three categories, Foundations, Modules, and Automation, based on the admin's mental model for better clarity.

03 · Design

System & high-fi

Created the component library and established a progress model, then designed each module based on that foundation.

04 · Validate

Prototype & handoff

Conducted usability tests on the hub and main workflows, refined them based on feedback, and delivered detailed specifications and design tokens to the engineering team.

Before

A flat A–Z list of settings

16 equally-weighted links. No hierarchy, no go-live path.

Decision

Three mental-model groups

Foundations → Modules → Automation, mirroring how a fleet comes online.

Before

No way to know how far along you were or what blocked go-live.

Decision

Progress ring, "needs attention" counts and per-item status.

Before

Show every module always

Customers saw config for products they hadn't bought.

Decision

Surface what's relevant

Modules reflect entitlements; "Set Up" vs a live summary signals state.

Before

Blind toggles for drivers

Admins configured the driver flow with no idea what drivers saw.

Decision

The driver app renders in real time beside the settings.

07Reflection

What I'd carry into the next role.

Structure beats screens.

Re-grouping the IA and adding a progress model moved the metrics more than any single screen. The mental model was the design.

Support tickets are research gold.

The most honest map of broken flows came from what admins were already calling in about.

Invest in the system early.

The component library slowed the first release and sped up all sixteen that followed, and it's what makes the product feel like one thing.
