The problem

Buying and servicing a conveyor lived in inboxes and phone calls.

Fluent engineers and manufactures heavy-duty conveyors for recycling and waste facilities, equipment that can't afford downtime. But once a machine shipped, everything that came after it was manual.

A

No single source of truth

Operators tracked assets, parts, and service history in spreadsheets or not at all.

B

Quotes by email, signed by PDF

Requesting parts, getting a quote, and signing it bounced between sales reps and printers.

C

Reactive, not preventive maintenance

Teams discovered a worn bearing only when a line went down costing throughput.

D

No visibility after ordering

"Where's my shipment?" meant another phone call to the dealer.

04 The platform

One connected flow, anchored to the asset.

The portal is organized around a single mental model: your assets are the home base, and every action, parts, quotes, payment, service, flows from them.

01 / Asset detail

Everything about a line, in one place.

Drill into any conveyor to see its full bill of parts. From here an operator can add a part to the cart, open a help-desk ticket against the asset, or log a maintenance task, without losing the context of which machine they're on.

  • ✓Searchable parts catalog per asset
  • ✓One tap to add to cart
  • ✓Raise a ticket or task in context

02 / Cart → Quote

A cart that becomes a quote.

Because conveyor parts are made-to-order and freight varies, the cart doesn't check out, it sends a quote request to the dealer. The customer keeps a running estimate while the dealer firms up freight and lead time.

  • ✓Parts grouped by the asset they belong to
  • ✓Request routes straight to Open Quotes

03 / Open Quotes

Compare, sign, and order in one motion.

Every request lands on the Open Quotes page. The customer can compare quotes, read the line-item breakdown, e-sign, and place the order in one motion. Signing converts the quote into a confirmed order and triggers the invoice.

  • ✓All quotes & their status in one list
  • ✓In-line e-signature → place order

04 / Invoice → Pay → Track

Pay the invoice, then watch it ship.

Placing an order generates an invoice on the Invoices page, where the customer picks which to pay. Payment fires a shipment notification, and the same order is now trackable end-to-end, no phone call required.

  • ✓Pay any open invoice in-app
  • ✓Live shipment tracking with ETA

05 / Maintenance

Stay ahead of downtime.

A dedicated maintenance page groups every task by urgency; due, upcoming, and late, so teams service equipment before it fails. Completing a task logs it back to the asset's history and reschedules the next interval.

  • ✓Late / due / upcoming, at a glance
  • ✓Complete a task → logs to asset history

What testing changed

Severity What we observed What we changed
High

Cart → quote felt like a dead end

Users built a cart but weren't sure how to turn it into a quote request.

 Added a persistent "Request quote" summary panel with a clear primary action on the cart itself.
High

E-signature was missed

Several operators scrolled past the signature step and tried to place the order.

 Made signing an explicit, blocking step with an inline sign-and-accept affordance before order placement.
Medium

Maintenance status read as decorative

Due / late chips weren't perceived as actionable.

 Promoted overdue items to the dashboard with count badges and a direct link to schedule.
Medium

Part-to-asset link unclear

Users weren't certain a part fit their specific machine.

 Anchored the parts catalog inside the asset and labeled compatibility explicitly.
Medium

Shipment ETA hard to scan

Tracking timeline was understood but ETA took a beat to find.

 Surfaced the next milestone & ETA at the top of the tracking panel.
